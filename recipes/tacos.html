Tacos

A brief history of tacos:

With roots tracing back to ancient civilizations in Mesoamerica, tacos have a vibrant and diverse history. Native peoples such as the Aztecs and Mayans utilized tortillas made from maize to wrap and hold various fillings, from meats to beans and vegetables. After the Spanish colonization, traditional Mexican ingredients merged with European influences, resulting in the taco's evolution. It wasn't until the 19th century that tacos began to gain prominence, with street vendors in Mexico City offering delicious and convenient handheld meals. As Mexican cuisine spread, so did the taco, adapting to different regions and tastes. In the present day, tacos are a global phenomenon, appreciated for their endless variety of fillings, salsas, and styles, reflecting the rich heritage and culinary creativity of Mexico.

Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons dried minced onion
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • ½ teaspoon cornstarch
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ½ teaspoon red pepper flakes (Optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon cayenne pepper (Optional)
  • ¼ teaspoon dried minced garlic
  • ¼ teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • ½ cup water

Steps:

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Mix minced onion, salt, chili powder, cornstarch, cumin, red pepper flakes, cayenne pepper, minced garlic, and oregano in a bowl; set seasoning mix aside.
  3. Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook and stir ground beef in the hot skillet until browned and crumbly, 7 to 10 minutes. Drain and discard any excess grease.
  4. Pour water and seasoning mix over beef; stir to combine. Bring to a simmer and cook until mixture thickens slightly, about 5 minutes.
